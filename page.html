Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 – 2015

“Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.”

Biography

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born in Rameswaram, he had a distinguished career in aerospace engineering, leading India's civilian space program and missile development efforts at ISRO and DRDO. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missiles and a key role in the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests, he was a widely respected figure and also known as the "People's President" for his focus on youth and public service His autobiography, Wings of Fire, remains a powerful testament to his journey from humble beginnings to national hero.
